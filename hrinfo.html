Paid Time Off (PTO)

Eligibility, accrual, requesting time off, and payout rules.

Important: PTO does not post until the last day of the month. Earned PTO hours appear on the last paycheck of the month.

All full- and part-time employees are eligible for PTO benefits. Part-time employees working 20–29 hours per week earn PTO on a prorated basis. Full-time employees work 30+ hours per week. PTO accrual begins on the first day of employment. PTO can be used only after it is earned. PTO will not be earned during an unpaid leave of absence.

To schedule PTO, employees should submit a request two weeks before the requested leave. Requests are approved based on factors including staffing requirements.

PTO is paid at the employee’s base rate at the time the leave is taken. PTO pay is not included in overtime calculation and does not include incentives/bonuses. If a holiday falls during vacation, the day is charged to holiday pay rather than PTO.

If employment is terminated, accrued unused PTO earned through the last day of active employment will be paid at the employee’s base rate of pay at termination.
